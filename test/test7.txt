Technology & Inventions
Bluetooth Name: The technology is named after a 10th-century Viking king, Harald Bluetooth, who united disparate Scandinavian tribes.
Microwave Accident: The microwave oven was invented by accident when a radar scientist noticed a chocolate bar had melted in his pocket.
Apple's Turning Point: The success of the iPod in 2001 is what primarily transformed Apple from a niche computer company into a global electronics giant.
Fastest Jet: NASA’s X-43 experimental plane currently holds the record for the fastest jet, reaching nearly 10 times the speed of sound.
Velcro Inspiration: Velcro was invented after an engineer noticed how burrs from a plant stuck to his dog’s fur during a walk.
Airbag Speed: A car's airbag deploys in just 0.04 seconds, which is significantly faster than the blink of a human eye.
3D Printing Advance: In 2025, 3D printing is increasingly being used to create complex human tissue scaffolds for medical research.
GPS History: Global Positioning System (GPS) technology was originally developed strictly for military use before being opened to the public.
Stethoscope Origin: The stethoscope began as a simple rolled-up paper tube invented by a doctor who was uncomfortable placing his ear on patients' chests.
D-Wave Intelligence: Some tech leaders predict that quantum-assisted intelligent machines will be capable of performing any human task by 2028.
