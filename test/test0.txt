General Curiosities & Myths
New Car Smell: The famous "new car smell" is actually a cocktail of over 200 different chemicals, including benzene and toluene.
Chainsaw Origins: Chainsaws were originally invented in the late 18th century as a medical tool to aid in difficult childbirths.
Bermuda Triangle: Statistical data shows the Bermuda Triangle is no more hazardous than any other heavily traveled ocean route.
Everest Height: Mount Everest is the highest point above sea level, but Mauna Kea in Hawaii is technically taller if measured from its underwater base.
Moon Shrinking: The Moon has shrunk in radius by about 150 feet over the last several hundred million years due to cooling of its interior.
Zero Calories: There is no such thing as "zero-calorie" food; even celery requires more energy to digest than it provides, but it still contains energy.
Lego Strength: A standard Lego brick can support the weight of 375,000 other bricks before it cracks.
World Maps: Most flat world maps (Mercator projection) are inaccurate; they make Greenland look roughly the same size as Africa when it is actually 14 times smaller.
Head Heat Myth: You do not lose most of your body heat through your head; covering any part of the body helps prevent heat loss equally.
Honey Preservation: Honey is naturally antimicrobial and never spoils; edible honey has been found in ancient Egyptian tombs thousands of years old.
