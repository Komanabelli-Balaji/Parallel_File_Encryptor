Pop Culture & Media
The Office: The US version of The Office was nearly canceled after its first season due to low ratings; it was saved partly by Steve Carell's success in The 40-Year-Old Virgin.
Wizard of Oz: The iconic song "Somewhere Over the Rainbow" was almost cut from the final version of the film because executives thought it slowed the pace.
Bugs Bunny Habits: Bugs Bunny's habit of chewing carrots while talking was inspired by a specific scene featuring Clark Gable in the 1934 film It Happened One Night.
Willy Wonka Funding: The 1971 movie was fully funded by the Quaker Oats Company specifically to promote a new line of Wonka-branded candy bars.
Coachella Origin: The Coachella festival was inspired by a 1993 Pearl Jam concert held at a polo club to boycott Ticketmaster-controlled venues.
Star Wars Royalties: Alec Guinness (Obi-Wan Kenobi) negotiated a 2.25% share of the film's royalties, which earned his family an estimated $95 million over his lifetime.

Back to the Future: The iconic clock tower lightning strike was only added to the script because the original "nuclear test site" ending was too expensive to film.
McDonald's Mascot: In 2016, McDonald's limited Ronald McDonald's public appearances due to a wave of creepy clown sightings across the United States.
